Create Your Hackathon

Step 1: Basic Information












Step 2: Communication

A common form of communication in Hackathons are Discord servers!

You can create an account and server here : Discord


Step 3: Prizes

A Major Part about Hackathons are what the participants are competing for!

It's important to give certificates/awards, to recognize the winning team for their hard work.

Step 4: Programming Languages/Software

What will the hackers be coding/working with?

A few examples of languages include Python, HTML, CSS, Java, Javascript

There's also other software to work with, such as Code.org or Scratch


Step 5: Judging

It's hard for one person to judge an entire competition, so it's recommended to bring a few friends to help you judge!

Hackers can give presentations and display video represenations on how their app functions, and what the purpose of it is.

It is recommended that you make a basic judging rubric, so you know what you're looking for.

Step 6: The Theme

All Hackathons have a Theme, so the Hackers know what to code for.

Usually Themes have an impact on the community

A few examples include Education, or Health themed.

Step 7: Hosting

Now that you have setup your own Hackathon, you need a place to host it!

Usually Hackathons can be hosted at public locations, like nearby schools, or colleges.

A few ways to get a hosting date include emailing the school adult staff, or contacting peers in your school's student body.

This step should be done a few months in advance, for a better chance to get an oppurtunity.

Step 8: Discord Server Setup

Earlier we created a Discord server, but now it's time to set it up!

Here is a template that you can upload to your server : Template

It would be recommended that you post video tutorials on usage of programming languages, software, or github in the preparing channel.

This can ensure that the students participating in your Hackathon aren't going in with any knowledge, and have a foundation on how to make their app and submit it.
